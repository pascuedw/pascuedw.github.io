About Max Fleischer

Max Fleischer was an animator, director, and inventor. He has created 30 patents for his animation productions. He was considered one of the three major pioneers of American animations. He later died from cardiovascular disease on September 11, 1972, in Woodland Hills, California.

His Achievements

Max and his brother, Dave Fleischer, had invented the Rotoscope. The Rotoscope allows the studio to create realistic animations. It's done by capturings flims that are frame-by-frame and captures movement from either an actor or object. Then the cartoonist will trace and animate the outline of the actor or object from the flim.

Before an Animator

Max Fleischer was born on July 19, 1883 in Krakow, Austro-Hungarian Empire. His father, William Fleischer, estabished and owned a successful tailoring shop. He attended public school and gradutated from Evening High School. Once he graduated, he attended The Machanics and Tradesmen School.
